Biography

A Decade of Healing Work

Keanne Owens is a Licensed Clinical Social Worker with Qualified Supervisor designation (LCSW-QS) based in Florida. With over ten years of dedicated practice, she has supported hundreds of individuals through some of life's most difficult challenges — including trauma, anxiety, depression, grief, identity, immigration stress, and relationship difficulties.

Keanne earned her Master of Social Work (MSW) degree and has since built a practice centered on the core belief that every person carries within them the seeds of their own healing. Her role as a therapist is not to fix, but to create the conditions in which growth naturally unfolds.

Her clinical background spans community mental health, private practice, and cross-cultural therapeutic work. She has a deep commitment to serving diverse populations, including immigrant communities, BIPOC clients, and first-generation Americans navigating the intersection of cultural identity and mental health.

Therapy Philosophy

Rooted in Connection & Evidence

Keanne's therapeutic approach is integrative — drawing on Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Trauma-Focused CBT, Motivational Interviewing, Attachment Theory, and mindfulness-based interventions. She tailors each approach to the individual, never applying a one-size-fits-all method.

Strength-Based

Therapy that builds on your existing resources and resilience, not just your difficulties.

Culturally Responsive

Deep sensitivity to how culture, identity, and lived experience shape your mental health.

Trauma-Informed

Safety, trust, and choice are embedded into every aspect of the therapeutic relationship.

Collaborative

You set the agenda. Keanne walks alongside you, not in front or behind you.

Supervision for RCSWI

Clinical Supervision

Keanne provides structured clinical supervision for Registered Clinical Social Work Interns (RCSWIs) in Florida pursuing licensure. As a Qualified Supervisor (QS), she offers both individual and group supervision formats tailored to support your clinical development, ethical grounding, and professional growth.

Supervision sessions cover case conceptualization, ethical dilemmas, clinical documentation, client engagement strategies, self-of-therapist work, and more. Keanne is committed to mentoring the next generation of compassionate, competent social work clinicians.

Immigration Psychological Evaluations

USCIS-Accepted Evaluations

Immigration psychological evaluations are professional clinical assessments used to support immigration petitions. Keanne conducts comprehensive evaluations for a variety of immigration cases, providing detailed, evidence-supported reports that meet USCIS standards and are accepted by immigration attorneys.

These evaluations require sensitivity, thoroughness, and cultural competency. Keanne brings all three — ensuring that your story is heard and documented with the depth and dignity it deserves.
